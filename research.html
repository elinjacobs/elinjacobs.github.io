Research Interests


I am an ecohydrologist interested in understanding how terrestrial ecosystems respond and contribute to global environmental change. My current work addresses climate change-related issues, primarily the effects of changing precipitation patterns on ecological and biogeochemical processes along with the scaling of these processes from point to grid to global scales. I am also interested in understanding and improving model representation of drought responses in plants to enable better predictions of the exchange of water and carbon between land and atmosphere in a changing climate. I use a combination of data synthesis, modeling and model-experiment integration approaches to address these issues.
